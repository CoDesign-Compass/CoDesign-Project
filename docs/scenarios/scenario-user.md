# Scenario 1: Improving access to regional family support services
## Actor: Mia, 35, a young single mother living in a suburb of Canberra
## Preconditions:
Mia receives a participation link forwarded by the community parenting group and clicks on it.

The system does not record any previous profiles; this is a one-time feedback process.
## Steps:
1. After accessing the link, Mia first enters the "Early Advertiser Profile Builder":

   She selects the tags: Single Mother, Area Resident, Family Support, Structured Preferences, Needs Clear Guidance

2. The system displays the issue: "Do you think family support services are available in the area?"

   Mia selects "Yes," and the system asks, "Why?"

   She responds, "It's difficult for me to make an appointment because they're only available on weekdays."

   The system then asks, "Why are weekdays inconvenient for you?" → "I don't have childcare."

   The system then asks, "Why am I unable to access childcare?" → "The cost is high, and there's no one to look after my children."

   When Mia enters "I don't know," the questioning ends.

3. Entering the "How?" stage, Mia browses the system's options:

   "Centralized Information Platform": Selects "Agree," noting, "It would be nice to be able to filter services by address."

   "Community Short-Term Care Pilot": Selects "Disagree," noting, "It's inconvenient to get there without transportation."

   “Multi-language Service Guides”: Selects “Disagree,” noting, “I’m okay with English, but this might help some of my neighbours.”

   “Extended Opening Hours on Weekends”: Selects “Agree,” noting, “I work full-time on weekdays, so evenings or weekends would help me a lot.”

   “Parent Peer Support Hotline”: Selects "Disagree," noting, “Sometimes I just want to talk to another mom who’s been through it. A support line run by parents would feel more relatable.”

4. On the final "Thank You" page, the system asked:

   "Would you like to leave your email address so we can participate in more co-creation activities in the future? (If you agree, we will associate your feedback with your profile for internal improvement purposes only.)"

   Mia selected "No" to continue participating anonymously.

   The system prompted: "Thank you for your feedback. Your feedback has helped us identify key needs."

## Postconditions:

  Mia's feedback and profile are stored anonymously and used only for topic clustering and system optimization.

  No email address or account history.

  A new profile will be required for future participation.

# Scenario 2: Improvement of mental health service process experience
## Actor (User): Jordan, 22, a college student, neurodiversity, and interested in policy and mental health issues.
## Preconditions:
   Jordan receives an email and clicks on the link.

   The system has no login portal; each entry starts by creating a new profile.
## Steps:
1. Upon entering the page, Jordan sees the prompt "Your voice is important to us!" and enters the profile builder:

   Select the tags: College Student, Neurodiversity, Mental Health, Logical Thinking, Likes Humour Guide

   The system generates a visual profile for this session only.

2. The system displays the issue: "Do you think the current university mental health service process has barriers?"

   Jordan selects "No"

   System asks: "Why?"

   Jordan responds: "I've used the online screening system before, and I think the process is pretty clear."

   The system continues: "Why do you think it's clear?"

   Jordan responds: "Because it uses a flowchart to show each stage, and you can make an appointment online."

   The system then asks: "Why does this process work for you?"

   Jordan responds:  "I don't like calling or talking to people in person, and it's easier for me to do it online."

   The system then asks: "Why?"

   Jordan responds: “Because I can take my time to read and respond. I don’t feel pressured or judged like I might in a face-to-face setting.”

   The system then asks: "Why?"

   Jordan responds: “I’m neurodivergent, and social interactions—especially spontaneous ones—can be overwhelming. Online forms give me control over the pace.”

3. Entering the "How?" stage, Jordan browses the system's options:

   “Streamlined Appointment Booking Page”: Selects “Agree,” noting, “It would be easier to follow if the steps were shown visually, like a flowchart.”

   “Automated Online Self-Assessment Tool”: Selects “Agree,” noting, “I prefer to reflect first before deciding to talk to someone—it gives me more control.”

   “Asynchronous Message-Based Support”: Selects “Agree,” noting, “I’d rather write things down when I’m calm and get a reply later instead of doing live chat or calls.”

   “Mental Health Info Integrated into Student Portal”: Selects “Agree,” noting, “If it were part of Moodle or my timetable, I’d actually notice it and click.”

   “Friendly Interface with a Guided Avatar”: Selects “Agree,” noting, “A supportive guide character would make the process feel less clinical and more like a conversation.”

4. A pop-up prompt appears on the end page:

   "Would you like to provide your email address to participate in future co-creation activities? If you agree, we will link your feedback to your profile for improvement purposes only."

   Jordan selects "Yes," enters his email address, and ticks the box to agree to the terms.

   The system confirms: "Your feedback has been linked to your profile. Thank you for your trust!"

## Postconditions:

   Jordan's email address is stored separately and associated with his profile with the user's permission.

   The system can invite Jordan back for the next event and reference his past contributions.
