# CoDesignCompass: Persona, Product Vision, and Project Introduction

## Persona

### User 1: Sophie
- **Name**: Sophie  
- **Age**: 27  
- **Role**: Part-time Community Support Officer & Primary Carer  
- **Background**: Lives in Melbourne with her younger brother, who has a chronic illness. Studied social sciences at university and is familiar with basic online tools (Google Docs, email, Zoom). Frequently uses government and NGO online services for health and social support information.  
- **Goals**: Share her lived experiences to help shape better policies; contribute actively to co-design initiatives.  
- **Motivations**: Provides a safe, structured way to share perspectives; can build a profile with tags and see real-time feedback; optional incentives (vouchers) and updates encourage ongoing participation.  
- **Frustrations/Pain Points**: Balancing caregiving duties with work; limited time for engagement; needs platforms that are easy to use and meaningful.

### User 2: Michael
- **Name**: Michael  
- **Age**: 63  
- **Role**: Retired Veteran  
- **Background**: Lives in Gold Coast; spends time gardening, fishing, and attending local RSL club activities. Education ended after high school; not very tech-savvy; uses iPad mainly for news, emails, and occasional forum browsing.  
- **Goals**: Contribute his experiences and perspectives on policy issues in a quick and anonymous way.  
- **Motivations**: Appreciates simple, no-login access; can answer questions without tracking or follow-up; able to share views efficiently.  
- **Frustrations/Pain Points**: Online systems often too complex; dislikes giving personal info; concerned about privacy and security; prefers straightforward, low-effort interaction.

### Admin: Olivia
- **Name**: Olivia  
- **Age**: 34  
- **Role**: System Administrator (only admin account)  
- **Background**: Olivia has a background in data science and works in Heidi Prowse focused on policy innovation. She has strong technical literacy, is comfortable with digital tools: dashboard analytics, survey systems, and content management platforms, but is not a professional developer. Her main focus is to ensure that issues (core problems and related “how/why” questions) are well-published, managed securely, and responses are collected reliably.  
- **Goals**:
  1. Collect authentic, diverse lived experience insights to inform policy and service design.
  2. Ensure feedback is anonymous, secure, and inclusive.
  3. Easily analyse responses through the dashboard (sentiment maps, word clouds, demographics).
  4. Identify emerging themes and gaps in lived experience voices.
  5. Reward participants fairly while protecting their privacy.
- **Motivations**:
  - Ensure smooth publishing of new issues without errors or overwriting existing content.
  - Collect high-quality participant responses that can be analyzed easily.
  - Maintain fairness and transparency by managing tags, anonymity, and time-limited links.
  - Use analytics insights to guide organizational decisions and engagement strategies.
  - Wants a real-time, transparent, and interactive feedback loop.
- **Frustrations/Pain Points**:
  - Traditional consultation processes are time-consuming, low engagement, and biased toward loudest voices.
  - Hard to synthesise qualitative data quickly into actionable insights.
  - Concerned about data privacy breaches and compliance with ethics.
  - Needs tools that work for non-digital natives (participants who may not be tech-savvy).
