## Project Introduction
### Background
Many policy frameworks and service pathways fail to fully reflect the experiences of those who use them. Individuals with lived experience often lack accessible, secure, and structured ways to provide meaningful feedback that can influence decision-making. This project was initiated to bridge this gap, giving users a safe and interactive platform to share their perspectives.

### Purpose
CoDesignCompass aims to empower individuals with lived experience to contribute directly to the design and improvement of policies and services. By guiding participants through reflective “why” and solution-oriented “how” questions, the app ensures that their insights are collected, analysed, and translated into actionable feedback for policymakers.

### Project Objectives
CoDesignCompass aims to provide a simple, secure, and user-friendly platform to empower diverse individuals to contribute to policy development. Key objectives include:
- Capturing deep insights into policy issues, identifying root causes and solutions.
- Highlighting emerging themes, divergent opinions, and underrepresented groups for targeted policy adjustments.
- Offering an admin dashboard with demographic, sentiment, and keyword analyses to inform policy reports.

### Core Features
- **Secure Web Access**: Mobile- and desktop-responsive, no login required, encrypted and privacy-compliant.
- **Profile Builder**: Users select demographic and identity tags, optionally add postcode and custom tags.
- **Why-How Exploration**: Structured reflection from “why” questions to solution exploration (“how”), with adaptive guidance.
- **Dynamic Questioning Engine**: Real-time analysis adjusts prompts, highlights gaps, and clusters emerging patterns.
- **Optional Rewards**: Optional email entry for rewards; fully separated from anonymous responses.
- **Admin Dashboard**: Visualises demographics, sentiment, themes, and engagement; allows filtering, exporting, and actionable insights.

### Target Users
- Engaged participants who are comfortable using online tools and want a structured, safe way to contribute detailed feedback, optionally track their input, and receive updates or small incentives.
- Privacy-conscious or casual participants who prefer quick, anonymous contributions without logging in or sharing personal information.
- Administrators and facilitators who need to manage secure publication of issues, collect anonymised responses reliably, and analyse patterns to identify emerging themes and gaps.
